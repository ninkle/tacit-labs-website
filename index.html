Tacit is the data layer that powers AI for Science.

We imagine a future in which AI is a powerful tool for scientific discovery, capable of helping us reason through complex problems and driving optimal decision-making across discovery and development pipelines.

Tacit collaborates with biotechs and academic labs to evaluate models on high-value, real-world tasks and works with frontier AI labs to systematically improve model performance on those tasks. Data collection will focus on outcome-verifiable, long-horizon planning and reasoning tasks to power the most critical functions in the life sciences

Powering the next-frontier of scientific discovery

Language models have gotten really good in certain domains, like coding and math. That's partly because there's so much clean, structured data (think of GitHub as a massive data repository; automated proof checkers as verifiers), and partly because everyone in ML already knows what "good" looks like in those fields. But in science (especially drug discovery) model capabilities are behind. Not because the models can't eventually get there, but because the right data and evaluation frameworks don't exist yet.

This is the core project at Tacit Labs — we're building the "frontier" of scientific tasks—basically, the kinds of problems that expert scientists actually care about, framed in a way that lets us evaluate how well models can reason through them. We're starting in a few specific modalities, namely small molecules and proteins, but would like to create data representing problems in many different modalities all across the discovery/development pipeline. At the end of the day, we're a data company focused on capturing expert reasoning in life sciences.
