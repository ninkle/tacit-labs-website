Tacit is the data layer that powers AI for Science.

We imagine a future in which AI is a powerful tool for scientific discovery, capable of helping us reason through complex problems and driving optimal decision-making across discovery and development pipelines.

Tacit collaborates with biotechs and academic labs to evaluate models on high-value, real-world tasks and works with frontier AI labs to systematically improve model performance on those tasks. Data collection will focus on outcome-verifiable, long-horizon planning and reasoning tasks to power the most critical functions in the life sciences

Powering the next frontier of scientific discovery

AI's greatest promise is advancing scientific discovery to transform what's possible for humanity. We haven't achieved this yet because of data scarcity. We need to measure far more: protein dynamics, reaction pathways, quantum effects, biological networks.

But the real bottleneck isn't just data quantity, it's that we've never captured how science actually gets done. So much of what makes research work lives in scientists' heads, not in papers. The analysis pipeline someone cobbled together that everyone now depends on. The instinct to adjust that one parameter. The ability to recognize when noise is actually signal. Scientists build this knowledge over years—learning which tools to trust, which questions matter, which hunches to follow.

Think about what really happens in research. A scientist doesn't just run experiments—they develop hunches, build mental models, learn which tools to trust and when. They know when Tuesday's weird result connects to something they read six months ago. They can sense which hypotheses are worth pursuing. This is the operating system of scientific discovery, and we've never properly documented it.

That's the gap we're filling. Not just measuring more, but capturing the full stack: the workflows, the decision-making, the hard-won expertise. Teaching AI systems not just to process scientific data but to develop scientific judgment. To generate hypotheses that matter. To know which experiments reveal truth and which just burn resources.

The next scientific revolution won't come from one brilliant lab or agent. It will come from the collective intelligence of thousands of scientists, captured and scaled. We exist to make that possible.
